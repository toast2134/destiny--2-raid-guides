Overview

Goal: Defend the relays to summon the consecrated mind.

This encounter introduces a new mechanic called the "Enlightened" Buff. Using the tether mechanic from last encounter, you tether from the floating boxes to the pillars. Once you tether, you will gain a buff called "Enlightened". Throughout the encounter, there will be vex with white shields that spawn. You must have the buff in order to shoot these shields off.

This encounter is relatively simple once you get a rhythm. There are 6 roles (one for everyone on the team). There are 4 relay defenders and 2 floaters. Illustrated in the image below, there are 4 relays. Each person must get to their relay to defend it. As you go around clearing ads, there will be anti-barier hob-goblins as you go around. These only spawn initially. The 2 floaters will move between the relays to refresh people's "Enlightenment" buff. After everyone is at a relay, hydras called "Angelics" will spawn at one of the four relays (there will be 3, one for each lane). Make sure to kill these whenever they spawn at a side. After they spawn at each relay, the middle barriers will open up. There is a final relay in the middle where you do the same: tether to get the buff, kill the angelics, and kill the ads with the shields. After 3 waves, you will be done.

Static Challenge: You can't tether if you have the Englightened buff.

Rotating Challenge: Everyone has to tether around the same time.

Tips/Tricks/Loadout Recommendations

  • The key to floating is the portals.
    • As shown in the picture, these portals link two relays.
    • They will activate once you have tethered to that specific relay.
  • There are several ways to call out the relays.
    • '
    • Starting at where the rally is, you go clockwise and count up from 1. So where the rally flag is 1. To the left is 2, diagonally across from 1 is 3, and to the right of 1 is 4.
    • You can also think of this like American baseball, where the rally flag is home base.
  • There are several ways to distribute everyone to each base. In general, one floater and the person who is staying at home base will stay at home base initially.
    • You can do the "drop off" method, where everyone else goes around to each base in a loop.
      1. Choose either left or right to go first.
      2. As you go, clear ads and tether at each location.
      3. Keep going until everyone has their relay.
      • With this method, if someone needs a buff, the floater that stayed can take the portals to fill in as the other floater is getting ready for their rotations.
    • You can do the "split" method (which tends to be faster).
      1. Have 2 people go left and 2 people go right.
        • The two people that go left, should be the one that stays at that relay, and one of the ones that will be going diagonally across (to the other side).
        • This is the same with right side.
      2. After each side does their thing, the two people meet up on the far side.
      3. After everyone is set up at a relay, you can get your rhythm and Enlightenment buffs timed.
  • It is important to be fast to clear each relay.
  • You only have to kill the Angelics since they will lock the tether box.
  • As you're setting up, you can leave the antibarriers to whoever is staying (especially if you need more time).
  • Anti-barrier weapons like Arbalest can help kill the Angelics.
  • For the static challenge, since the team is already sort of split into two teams of 3 (1 floater + 2 relay defenders), you just need to time your buffs with your "team".
  • For the rotating challenge, make sure everyone can tether, then just count down. Keep in mind that everyone can take portals, not just the floaters.
